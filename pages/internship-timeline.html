Internships: How to Find Them & When to Apply

Your roadmap to landing an internship in any business field

What is an Internship?

An internship is a short-term work experience offered by companies and other organizations for peopleâ€”usually studentsâ€”to get some entry-level exposure to a particular industry or field.

Benefits of Internships

  • Real-world experience: Apply classroom concepts to actual business problems and decision-making
  • Skill development: Gain proficiency in industry-specific software, tools, and professional skills
  • Career exploration: Confirm whether a particular career path is right for you
  • Professional network: Build relationships with mentors and colleagues who can help your career
  • Job offers: Many internships convert to full-time offersâ€”some companies hire 50-80% of their interns
  • Resume builder: Internship experience makes you significantly more competitive for post-graduation roles
  • Competitive advantage: Students with internships receive more job offers and higher starting salaries

Your Internship Timeline

Plan your path from freshman year to your dream internship

Freshman Year
Fall Semester

Focus: Build foundation and explore

  • Complete 16 credit hours with strong GPA
  • Join major-specific clubs and organizations
  • Attend COBA career events and workshops
  • Start building LinkedIn profile
  • Meet with COBA Professional Development team
Freshman Year
Spring Semester

Focus: Develop skills and start networking

  • Complete 16 credit hours, maintain strong GPA
  • Gain relevant certifications for your major
  • Attend career fair (practice for next year)
  • Build technical skills (Excel, software, etc.)
  • Connect with alumni on LinkedIn
Freshman Year
Summer

Focus: Gain any experience

  • Work any job (shows work ethic and responsibility)
  • Take online courses relevant to your major
  • Read industry publications and books
  • Network with family/friends in your field
  • Volunteer for organizations related to your interests
Sophomore Year
Fall Semester

Focus: Prepare for recruiting season

  • Complete 16 credit hours, keep GPA strong
  • Join major-specific organizations (Investment Fund, ENACTUS, etc.)
  • Perfect your resume with help from COBA team
  • Start researching companies and positions
  • Practice interview skills and elevator pitch
Sophomore Year
Spring Semester

Focus: Apply aggressively

  • Complete 16 credit hours
  • Attend career fair - apply to 20+ companies
  • Apply online to 50+ internships
  • Network with alumni on LinkedIn
  • Practice interviews constantly
  • Follow up on all applications
Sophomore Year
Summer

Junior Year
Fall Semester

Focus: Junior year recruiting (most critical)

Important Note:

Although you can have as many internship experiences as you'd like, many students will choose to pursue their internship the summer before their senior year (junior year summer). However, the more internships you complete, the more competitive you'll be. Many top students complete 2-3 internships before graduation.

Application Timeline by Major:

Finance/Accounting: Applications often open in August-September for the following summer

Tech/CS: Rolling applications throughout the year, peak in fall

Marketing: Spring recruiting is common, but apply year-round

Consulting/Management: Structured recruiting in fall for next summer
